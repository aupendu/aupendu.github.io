Iterative Transmission and Atmospheric Light Update based Recurrent Neural Network for Single Image Dehazing

Aupendu Kar, Sobhan Kanti Dhara, Debashis Sen, Prabir Kumar Biswas

Department of Electronics and Electrical Communication Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, India

 Abstract

Hazy images suffer from the visibility of the scene due to various environmental effects. Recent advancements in image dehazing propose different haze removal approaches, but their performances degrade with the increase of haze and color cast. It is always desirable to have a dehazing model that works across a wide variety of hazy conditions. To address this, we propose an Iterative Prior Updated Dehazing Network (IPUDN), a recurrent convolutional neural network for progressive haze removal. We estimate and iteratively update the priors for dehazing. We estimate the transmission map and atmospheric light intensity as prior information to the dehazing network. These priors help to reduce the burden on the dehazing network while performing end-to-end training. Our transmission map and atmospheric light updater networks iteratively update the transmission map and atmospheric light intensity, which prevent the significant performance drop while increasing the haze density and also produce visually pleasant outputs. We also address the color cast problem and propose a novel convolutional architecture to estimate atmospheric light intensity to handle the same. Our prior updater based recursive mechanism without recurrent block performs significantly better (upto ~3.4 dB boost up) than LSTM based recurrent mechanism. The fusion of both LSTM and prior updater mechanism further increases the performance of the dehazing framework. To assess our model's full capacity, we experimentally validate our results in a wide range of hazy conditions, and our approach outperforms recently developed state-of-the-art techniques by considerable margins. We also evaluate our network's performance in real-world hazy images, and experimental evidence confirms its superiority over other methods.
